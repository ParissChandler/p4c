Tips

1. At the beginning of the converstaion, if you are in a hurry and want to get the most important information out, let the other side know that have limited time. Schedule to discuss more of the conversation later.

2. You cannot control what other people think or want, but you can control what you want. Make sure to be positive about what it is that you want to accomplish from the conversation. Be sure that this accomplishment is the root of the problem.

3. Do not be afraid to share your needs with the other side. After all, how will they know to provide you with the correct answers if you do not let your needs be known. Don't assume that they can read signals.

4. Ask the other side what they want. You'd be surprised by how honest and open people can be when you make them feel comfortable and safe enough to be so.

5. Second Best - think about other alternatives to the situation in case the conversation will not work. Then pick only one option that seemsmost reasonable and attractive. This is your second best. Try to improve it so it will be even more attractive. Think for a moment, how you would feel walking into a conversation with a backup idea. Having a plan B in case your plan A falls short, makes you feel more prepared, confident, and powerful.

6. Even if you disagree - try to listen to the other side. Sometimes people just want to be heard which can make all the difference.

7. Try to let the other side understand that you both have a similar goal. Even if there is a gap in your desires/thoughts, etc., you both still want to solve the problem.

8. Sometimes the other side just wants a sincere apology. It can be more valuable than any compensation.

9. As long as you are happy with the goals you set for yourself, it will be a successful conversation. There are no winners or losers.

10. There are no rules. Each dispute can be solved in multiple ways. Get creative!
